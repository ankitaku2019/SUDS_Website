Family Independence Initiative: Attrition and Retention

Project Overview

This project aims to understand the effectiveness of the non-profit organization, Family Independence Initiative (FII), around how long households engage with FII for and what characteristics or patterns are linked to achieving household goals and improving outcomes. Students used K-means clustering to effectively visualize the statistical data they received and provide appropriate measures to the client.

Client Information

Family Independence Initiative (FII) trusts and invests directly in low-income families across the nation so they can work individually and collectively to achieve prosperity. Families that partner with FII share data about their finances, goals, community engagement, and other measures of well being. FII then uses that data to learn directly from families what works for them.

Challenges Faced & Lessons Learned

There were seven datasets with varying degress of completeness and different answers being sought from each of the datasets. Several datasets were difficult to create data visualizations from since they had inconsistent labeling and missed large gaps of information. Thus, it was especially important to ensure that the data visualizations we presented were honest, and clearly communicated how the gaps of data might affect or bias the results in either a positive or negative way.
